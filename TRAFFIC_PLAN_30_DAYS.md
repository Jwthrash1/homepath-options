# HomePath Options 30-Day Traffic Plan

This plan is built to get low-cost homeowner traffic without making risky mortgage promises. The core idea is simple: publish helpful homeowner education, route every post to HomePathOptions.com, and turn the website into the next step for people who want to compare mortgage, refinance, home equity, HELOC, or reverse mortgage conversations with licensed professionals.

Important launch note: do not push real traffic until the CRM or backup lead delivery is turned back on and a test lead works from a phone.

## Positioning

Primary hook:

Check your home financing options in a few minutes.

Best content theme:

Home Equity Checkup

Why this works:

People do not usually wake up wanting to fill out a lead form. They do wake up wondering how to pay for repairs, reduce payment pressure, consolidate debt, use home equity, or understand whether refinancing still makes sense.

Use these phrases often:

- Explore your options
- Talk with a licensed mortgage professional
- Learn what questions to ask before comparing offers
- Estimate your equity
- Understand possible next steps
- No obligation to continue

Avoid these phrases:

- Guaranteed approval
- Lowest rates
- Bad credit OK
- Cash today
- Free money
- Government benefit
- Everyone qualifies
- We lower your payment
- Skip your lender

## 30 Short Video Scripts

Each video should be 20 to 45 seconds. End each one with: "You can start with a quick option check at HomePathOptions.com."

1. 3 things homeowners misunderstand about HELOCs

Hook: "A HELOC is not free money. Here are 3 things homeowners should know."

Script: "First, a HELOC is a loan secured by your home. Second, the amount you may be able to access depends on equity, credit, income, property, and lender guidelines. Third, it may be useful for some goals but not every goal. Before comparing options, understand your home value, mortgage balance, and timeline."

2. The simple equity calculation

Hook: "Homeowners, this simple number matters."

Script: "Take your estimated home value and subtract what you owe on your mortgage. That gives you a rough equity estimate. It is not a loan approval, but it helps you know what conversation to have next."

3. HELOC vs refinance

Hook: "Should you look at a HELOC or a refinance?"

Script: "A refinance may replace your current mortgage. A HELOC may add a separate line of credit secured by your home. The right conversation depends on your current rate, equity, credit, payment goals, and how you plan to use the funds."

4. Three things to know before comparing offers

Hook: "Do not compare home equity options until you know these 3 things."

Script: "Know your home value, your mortgage balance, and your goal. Are you repairing the home, consolidating debt, handling cash flow, or planning a major expense? Different goals may need different loan conversations."

5. Reverse mortgage basics

## Compliance Notes

Mortgage, housing, and credit ads need careful wording. Google says financial product advertisers must comply with state and local regulations for targeted locations, and disclosures should help users make informed financial decisions. TikTok's HEC policy says U.S. or Canada housing, employment, or credit advertisers must self-declare the special ad category, and targeting options like age, gender, ZIP code, marital or parental status, and protected-characteristic-related categories are restricted.

Use educational language. Do not imply guaranteed financing, special approval, government affiliation, credit repair, loan modification guarantees, or immediate cash.

Sources:

- Google Ads Financial products and services policy: https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/2464998
- TikTok Housing, Employment, and Credit Ad Policy: https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/housing-employment-credit-hec-ad-policy
