# Homeowner Equity Checklist

## 7 Questions To Ask Before Comparing HELOCs, Refinancing, Or Home Equity Options

Home equity can be useful, but it is important to understand your numbers, your goals, and the tradeoffs before making a financing decision.

HomePath Options is not a lender, mortgage broker, or loan originator. We help homeowners start the conversation and may connect them with licensed mortgage professionals who can discuss available options.

## 1. What is my main goal?

Before comparing loan options, write down what you are trying to accomplish.

Common goals include:

- Home repairs
- Debt consolidation
- Cash flow planning
- Home improvement
- Buying another home
- Refinancing an existing mortgage
- Retirement planning

Different goals may lead to different financing conversations.

## 2. What is my estimated home value?

Start with a reasonable estimate of what your home may be worth.

You can use:

- Recent comparable home sales
- Online home value estimates
- A real estate professional's opinion
- A formal appraisal, if needed later

Online estimates can be helpful, but they are not final loan decisions.

## 3. What do I owe on my current mortgage?

Your mortgage balance is one of the most important numbers in a home equity conversation.

Simple estimate:

Estimated home value minus mortgage balance equals estimated equity.

Example:

Home value: $450,000  
Mortgage balance: $200,000  
Estimated equity: $250,000

This is not an approval. It is only a starting point.

## 4. How soon do I need to make a decision?

Your timeline matters.

Ask yourself:

- Is this urgent?
- Can I compare options carefully?
- Is this for a future project?
- Do I need funds for a repair, remodel, or debt strategy?

Rushed decisions can lead to poor comparisons.

## 5. What monthly payment can I realistically handle?

Do not focus only on how much you may be able to borrow.

Also consider:

- Monthly payment
- Interest rate
- Fees and closing costs
- Loan term
- Whether the payment can change
- Total cost over time

For example, some HELOCs may have variable rates and changing payments.

## 6. How long do I plan to stay in the home?

Your expected timeline in the home can affect the conversation.

If you plan to stay long term, one option may make sense. If you may sell soon, costs and timing may matter more.

## 7. What are the risks?

Many home financing options are secured by your home.

That means the decision deserves careful thought.

Ask:

- What happens if my income changes?
- What happens if rates change?
- What are the fees?
- What happens if I sell the home?
- Am I replacing unsecured debt with debt secured by my home?
- Have I spoken with a licensed professional?
