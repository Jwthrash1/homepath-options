## Post 2

Simple homeowner math:

Estimated home value minus mortgage balance equals estimated equity.

That number is not a loan approval, but it is a smart place to begin before comparing home financing options.

## Post 3

HELOC, refinance, home equity loan, reverse mortgage.

These are not the same thing.

Different goals may require different conversations. Home repairs, debt consolidation, cash flow, remodeling, and retirement planning can each change the discussion.

## Post 4

A reverse mortgage is a loan, not free money.

For older homeowners, it may be worth discussing with a licensed professional, but it has rules, costs, and long-term considerations.

Start with education before making a decision.

## Post 6

If monthly payments feel heavy, do not rush into a decision.

A refinance, HELOC, or home equity option may be worth discussing, but costs, risks, and long-term impact matter.

## Post 8

Before you compare home equity options, ask:

What is my goal?
What is my home worth?
What do I owe?
How soon do I need funds?
What payment can I handle?
What are the risks?
